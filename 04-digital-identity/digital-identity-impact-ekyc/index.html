    How Digital Identity Changes eKYC¶

    Definition¶

    This article analyzes how the transition to digital identity ‚Äî verifiable credentials, wallets, and government APIs ‚Äî will reshape the eKYC industry, what technology becomes obsolete, and what new opportunities emerge.

    What Disappears vs What Remains¶

    eKYC Component Impact of Digital Identity
    Document capture ‚¨áÔ∏è Reduced ‚Äî credentials presented digitally
    OCR / text extraction ‚¨áÔ∏è Eliminated ‚Äî structured data from credential
    Document classification ‚¨áÔ∏è Eliminated ‚Äî credential type is metadata
    Document forensics ‚¨áÔ∏è Reduced ‚Äî cryptographic verification replaces forensic analysis
    Face matching (doc vs selfie) ‚¨áÔ∏è Reduced ‚Äî biometric bound to credential
    Face liveness ‚û°Ô∏è Remains ‚Äî still needed to prove holder is present
    Sanctions/PEP screening ‚û°Ô∏è Remains ‚Äî still required by regulation
    Risk assessment ‚û°Ô∏è Remains ‚Äî risk-based approach still mandatory
    Ongoing monitoring ‚û°Ô∏è Remains ‚Äî AML/KYT obligations unchanged
    Credential verification ‚¨ÜÔ∏è New ‚Äî verify issuer signatures, check revocation
    Wallet integration ‚¨ÜÔ∏è New ‚Äî accept credentials from EUDI/other wallets
    Credential issuance ‚¨ÜÔ∏è New ‚Äî issue verified credentials after KYC

    Strategic Implications for eKYC Providers¶

    Opportunity Details
    Credential verification New service: verify EUDI/VC credentials for clients
    Credential issuance After completing KYC, issue reusable credential to customer
    Wallet infrastructure Build/operate wallet systems for governments or enterprises
    Orchestration Route between document eKYC and credential eKYC based on availability
    Compliance layer Screening (sanctions, PEP, adverse media) remains regardless of identity method
    Risk Details
    Disintermediation If government provides identity directly, middleware value decreases
    Revenue compression Credential verification is cheaper than document processing
    Skill shift Cryptography + protocol expertise vs computer vision + OCR

    Key Takeaways¶

    Summary

    • Digital identity will gradually replace document-centric eKYC ‚Äî but the transition takes 5-10+ years
    • Face liveness remains essential ‚Äî proving the credential holder is present
    • Screening (sanctions, PEP, AML) is unaffected ‚Äî still required by law
    • New opportunities: credential issuance, wallet infrastructure, orchestration
    • New risk: disintermediation if governments provide identity directly to institutions
    • Smart eKYC providers will offer both document and credential verification paths
